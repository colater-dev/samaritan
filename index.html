Our Vision

Urine is the body's most accessible real-time diagnostic signal

Unlike blood draws that require invasive sampling, urine is continuously produced and naturally collected in catheterized patients. It carries a rich constellation of biomarkers — and no one is systematically reading them.

Samaritan is building AI models that decode this signal in real-time. Our first application is Acute Kidney Injury prediction. Our ambition is far greater — a comprehensive biomarker intelligence platform for the human body.

The Problem

The standard of care hasn't changed in decades

Today, ICU nurses manually measure urine output every hour — a process that is error-prone, labour-intensive, and provides data far too late to prevent organ damage. By the time AKI is detected through conventional methods, the window for effective intervention has often already closed.

Manual Measurement

Hourly readings by hand. Subjective, inconsistent, and takes nurses away from critical care.

Late Detection

Creatinine-based diagnosis catches AKI hours or days after damage has already begun.

Preventable Deaths

Earlier detection — even by hours — dramatically improves outcomes and reduces mortality.

How It Works

Three steps to earlier detection

AroBag transforms the ICU's most overlooked data source into a predictive intelligence system.

Monitor

AroBag continuously collects and measures urine output, specific gravity, and key biomarkers from catheterised ICU patients.

Detect

AI models analyse patterns in real-time, identifying the earliest signals of Acute Kidney Injury or Chronic Kidney Disease.

Alert

Clinicians receive actionable, timely alerts through the companion app and hospital EMR — enabling faster intervention.
