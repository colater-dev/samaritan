Visually strong but flagged as "too much information" — requires too much reading to understand what they're doing. We'll draw from the renal care visual language and product photography, but avoid their information density. Neuro-Sonic's one-thing-at-a-time approach is the corrective.

06 — IP Considerations

What We Can and Can't Say

IP concerns limit public disclosure until clinical trials complete. The website must be compelling without revealing specifics that larger players could copy.

Safe to Communicate

  • The problem (AKI statistics, manual measurement)
  • The vision (foundational model for human body)
  • The form factor (smart urine bag)
  • AI-powered prediction (general capability)
  • Cost advantage positioning
  • Team credentials and affiliations
  • "Patent pending" status

Avoid Until Trials Complete

  • Specific technical architecture details
  • Exact biomarker measurement methods
  • AI model specifics (architecture, training data)
  • Specific accuracy metrics (unless approved)
  • Detailed hardware specifications
  • Anything that could be reverse-engineered by Fize ($30M) or Retia ($76M)

This constraint actually helps the messaging: the landing page leads with vision and market opportunity (investor-friendly), while the product page stays at benefit-level language (clinician-friendly) without exposing technical IP.
