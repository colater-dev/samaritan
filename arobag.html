How It Works

From collection to clinical action

AroBag transforms routine urine collection into an intelligent monitoring system in four seamless steps.

Step 1

Collect & Monitor

AroBag replaces the standard urine collection bag, continuously measuring urine output, specific gravity, and key biomarkers from catheterised ICU patients — with zero workflow disruption.

Step 2

Digitise with Computer Vision

The companion app uses computer vision to digitally capture and transmit measurements — eliminating manual reading errors and automating data entry.

Step 3

Analyse & Predict

AI models analyse biomarker patterns in real-time, comparing against thousands of data points to detect early signals of AKI and CKD — hours before traditional creatinine-based diagnosis.

Step 4

Alert & Integrate

Predictive alerts are delivered to clinicians via the companion app and integrated directly into the hospital's EMR system — enabling faster, data-driven intervention.

Clinical Context

The science behind early detection

AI-based AKI prediction is backed by a growing body of clinical research. AroBag builds on this foundation with continuous, real-time biomarker monitoring.

AKI affects 20–50% of ICU patients

Acute Kidney Injury is one of the most common critical complications in intensive care, with incidence rates varying by patient population and severity of illness.

50%+ mortality in severe cases

Severe AKI carries extraordinarily high mortality rates, making early detection and intervention one of the highest-impact opportunities in critical care.

Hours matter for outcomes

Research consistently demonstrates that earlier detection of AKI — even by just a few hours — dramatically improves patient outcomes and reduces the need for costly interventions like dialysis.

Urine biomarkers are increasingly validated

A growing body of research validates urine-based biomarkers (TIMP-2, IGFBP7, NGAL) as early AKI indicators — supporting the scientific basis for continuous urine monitoring.
